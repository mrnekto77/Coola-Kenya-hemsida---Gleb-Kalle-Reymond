East African Arts Festival

East African Arts Festival

Each year on March the Nairobi city hosts the East African Arts Festival. This which is known as a competition and is the biggest of its kind in the region. It attracts competitors and spectators from all around the world to compete in these events. The event itself runs for three days and showcases art, music, theater, fashion, litterature, architecture, sculpture and traditional crafts. It is also known to be hosted by the Kenyan National Museum.

Kenyan Easter

Easter in Kenya

When it comes to Easter in Kenya. They have things called Good Friday and Easter Monday which are their public holidays. These are supposed to mark the long weekend commemorating Jesus Christ’s crucifixion and resurrection. Good Friday often shows a big procession through the streets with dramatic recreations of the Stations of the Cross. This which is culminating in church services. This leads on to the saturday where there is involvement with a bonfire outside the church and where you will be able to light candles and be able to prey inside. This leads on to the sunday where there is feasts marked by singing, bell-ringing, church services and family get togethers.

Eid al-Fitr

Eid al-Fitr

Eid Al Fitr is an Islamic celebration that takes place in september. Where the sighting of the moon is supposed to mark the end of the muslim holy month of fasting during ramadan. Eid celebrations usually always involve personal cleansing. This with communal prayers, charity donations and three full days of feasting and spending time with friends and family. These celebrations are the biggest along the coastal areas where most of the Muslim population reside in Kenya.

International Camel Derby and Festival

International Camel Derby and Festival

The annual International Camel Derby and Festival has been held since 1990 on the outskirts of Maralal town in the northern parts of Kenya. The derby itself usually takes place in August. The main attraction is the camel racing competitions also known as a feature of the event. The racing takes place over several days over and trough semi-desert regions and is open for almost everyone which includes amateurs and novices. Visitors come from all over the world to take part or watch the racing take place. Other than the camel races there is bicycle races, donkey rides, children entertainment and the opportunity to rent your own camel for a day.

Mombasa Carnival

Mombasa Carnival

During November is when the city of Mombasa celebrates a Kenyan culture with a carnival by the Indian Ocean. The kinds of people that come to this event is everything from Artists, musicians and tribal people who all flock to take part in the concerts that are considered one if the biggest annual events. One of the major things to take part in is the main street parade with floats that showcase the different tribal identities across the nation of Kenya. There is also street stalls and opportunities for drinking, eating and dancing.

Jamhuri Day

Jamhuri Day

Jamhuri means “republic” in Swahili and on December 12 is set aside as a public holiday to celebrate Kenya becoming a republic in 1964. The date itself is twice as important because the country also received independence from Britain on December 12, 1963. The occasion is marked as usual with dancing, parades and a speech from each of the eight provincial Presidents. Many towns also host cultural performances. While individual families often get together for meals and a good time. Fireworks are also often highlighted and air shows have become more popular in todays more modern times.

Kenyan Christmas

Kenyan Christmas

Kenyan christmas is as in many other countries to celebrate the birth of Jesus Christ on December 25. This which is a public holiday. The events around the country usually are religious in nature. Featuring church services, caroling and nativity performances. Both houses and churches are decorated with things like balloons, flowers and green leaves and the storefronts in the larger towns and cities are covered with fake snow. If the parents can afford gifts for their children it mostly consists of books, practical items or maybe a new outfit for attending church. Families get together to attend services (church) and then enjoy a feast which most definitely consists of roasted goat.

Kenyan New Year

Kenyan New Year

Kenyans also like many other countries celebrate new year but their celebrations begin the evening of December 31 with parties, music and church services. Leading up to midnight a countdown which sees fireworks, music and cheering to welcome in the new year. The church services and non-religious parties take place everywhere in the country and nation. With Many of these continuing all the way after dawn. Nairobi has one of the biggest new year events. With musical performances and massive firework displays. Mombasa meanwhile is well known for its new year beach parties. Those who often are hosted by local radio stations which pay to have live music and DJs.
